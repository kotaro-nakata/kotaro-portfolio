# Bringing engineering closer to real needs

In the Prosthetic Hand Project, I joined an effort to apply engineering knowledge to a social challenge. It was not enough to design and fabricate parts. We had to think about what would actually be meaningful for the person using it.

## Prototyping and Improvement

Design, fabrication, checking, and improvement repeated over time. We had to consider strength, weight, ease of movement, appearance, and maintenance together.

## Where Technology Points

What stayed with me is that technical quality is not decided by specs alone. Who uses it, in what situation, and with what feeling? Only after imagining that can you judge whether a design is good.
